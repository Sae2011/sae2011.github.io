Introduction

Senegal is located in Western Africa, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean, Mauritania, Mali, Guinea, and Guinea-Bissau.

The country has a population of over 16 million people and its capital, Dakar, is a major hub for trade and culture.

Geography

Location

Senegal is located in Western Africa, bordering the North Atlantic Ocean, between Guinea-Bissau and Mauritania.

Climate

The climate is generally tropical, with a hot and humid rainy season from May to November and a cooler dry season from December to April.

Natural Resources

Senegal's natural resources include fish, phosphates, and iron ore.

People and Society

Ethnic Groups

The largest ethnic group in Senegal is the Wolof, making up over 40% of the population, followed by the Fula and the Serer.

Languages

French is the official language, while Wolof, Pulaar, Jola, and Mandinka are also widely spoken.

Religions

Islam is the predominant religion in Senegal, with over 90% of the population being Muslim.
